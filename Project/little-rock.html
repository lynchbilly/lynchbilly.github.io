Little Rock

Little Rock, the capital and most populous city of Arkansas, is located on the banks of the Arkansas River. It serves as a major cultural, economic, and transportation hub for the state.

  • Founded: 1821 [1]
  • County: Pulaski County [2]
  • Population: 202,591 (2020) [3]
  • Area: 116.8 sq mi [2]

Things to Do in Little Rock

  • Visit the Clinton Presidential Center.
  • Explore the River Market District.
  • Tour the Arkansas State Capitol Building.
  • Discover art at the Arkansas Museum of Fine Arts.
  • Learn about history at the Little Rock Central High School National Historic Site.

History of Little Rock

The area that would become Little Rock was first noted by French explorer Jean-Baptiste Bénard de la Harpe in 1722, who referred to a small rock formation on the Arkansas River as "la Petite Roche" (the little rock). The town was officially founded in 1821 and became the capital of Arkansas in 1836. [1]
