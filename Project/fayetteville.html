Fayetteville

Fayetteville, home to the flagship campus of the University of Arkansas, is a lively city nestled in the Ozark Mountains. It's known for its vibrant arts and entertainment scene, outdoor recreational opportunities, and a strong sense of community.

City Classification

Fayetteville is best classified as a college town and regional urban center. Its identity is significantly shaped by the University of Arkansas, contributing to a youthful demographic and a lively cultural atmosphere, while also serving as a commercial and cultural hub for Northwest Arkansas.

  • Founded: 1829 [1]
  • County: Washington County [2]
  • Population: 93,949 (2020 Census) [2]
  • Area: 54.42 sq mi (land) [2]

Economic Overview

The median household income in Fayetteville is approximately $59,074 (2023). This is slightly higher than the state median household income for Arkansas ($58,773), reflecting a robust economy driven by the university, healthcare, and a growing business sector.

Things to Do in Fayetteville

  • Visit the University of Arkansas campus.
  • Explore Dickson Street for nightlife, dining, and entertainment.
  • Wander through the Botanical Garden of the Ozarks.
  • Attend a Razorback sporting event.
  • Hike or bike on the many trails, including the Razorback Greenway.
  • Browse local art galleries and boutiques on the historic Downtown Square.

History of Fayetteville

Originally known as Washington, the town was founded in 1829 and renamed Fayetteville in 1829 to avoid confusion with another Washington in the state. It was incorporated in 1836. The establishment of the Arkansas Industrial University (now the University of Arkansas) in 1871 cemented Fayetteville's role as a center for education and culture in the region.
