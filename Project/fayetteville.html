Fayetteville

Fayetteville is a vibrant city in Northwest Arkansas, known for being the home of the University of Arkansas. It offers a blend of college-town energy, natural beauty, and a thriving arts and culture scene.

  • Founded: 1829 [1]
  • County: Washington County [2]
  • Population: 93,949 (2020) [3]
  • Area: 57.4 sq mi [2]

Things to Do in Fayetteville

  • Visit the University of Arkansas campus.
  • Explore Dickson Street and its entertainment.
  • Enjoy the scenic beauty of the Botanical Garden of the Ozarks.
  • Hike or bike on the Razorback Greenway.
  • Discover local art at galleries and the Walton Arts Center.

History of Fayetteville

Fayetteville was established in 1829 and named after Fayetteville, Tennessee, the hometown of some of its early settlers. The city's early history is well-documented by the Fayetteville History Museum. [1] The city's growth has been closely tied to the University of Arkansas, founded in 1871.

Historical Event Date Description
Town Established 1829 Fayetteville was founded and named after Fayetteville, TN. [1]
University of Arkansas Founded 1871 The University of Arkansas was established, significantly shaping the city's development.
Designation as a "First Class City" 1883 Fayetteville was incorporated as a "First Class City".
