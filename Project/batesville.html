Batesville

Batesville, located in Northeast Arkansas along the White River, is the county seat of Independence County. It boasts a rich history as one of the oldest cities in Arkansas.

  • Founded: Early 1800s [1]
  • County: Independence County [2]
  • Population: 10,384 (2020) [3]
  • Area: 11.1 sq mi [2]

Things to Do in Batesville

  • Explore the historic downtown area.
  • Visit the Independence County Historical Society Museum.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities along the White River.
  • Discover local shops and restaurants.
  • Attend events at the Batesville Civic Center.

History of Batesville

Batesville holds the distinction of being one of Arkansas's oldest cities, with its roots tracing back to the early 19th century. Its strategic location along the White River contributed to its early growth as a trading and transportation hub. More detailed historical information can be found on the Encyclopedia of Arkansas. [1]

Historical Event Date Description
Early Settlement Early 1800s Batesville began as an early settlement along the White River. [1]
Incorporation January 9, 1838 Batesville was officially incorporated.
Growth as a River Port 19th Century The White River played a crucial role in Batesville's early economic development.
