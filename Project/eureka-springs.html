Eureka Springs

Eureka Springs is a unique and charming city nestled in the Ozark Mountains of Northwest Arkansas. Known for its Victorian architecture, natural springs, and vibrant arts scene, it offers a distinctive experience.

  • Founded: 1879 [1]
  • County: Carroll County [2]
  • Population: 2,073 (2020) [3]
  • Area: 6.4 sq mi [2]

Things to Do in Eureka Springs

  • Explore the historic downtown and its unique shops.
  • Visit the natural springs and caves.
  • Experience the Passion Play.
  • Discover local art galleries and craft shops.
  • Take a ride on the Eureka Springs & North Arkansas Railway.

History of Eureka Springs

Eureka Springs was founded in 1879 following the reported healing powers of its natural springs. It quickly became a popular resort town, attracting visitors with its unique landscape and Victorian architecture. The Encyclopedia of Arkansas provides a comprehensive history of the city. [1]

Historical Event Date Description
Discovery of Springs 1878 Dr. Alvah Jackson claimed the springs had healing powers, leading to the town's development.
Town Founded 1879 Eureka Springs was officially founded. [1]
Peak as a Resort Town Late 19th Century Eureka Springs flourished as a major health and tourist destination.
